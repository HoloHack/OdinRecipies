Tirami
sù

Prep Time 30 min
Chill Time 6 hrs (overnight)
Serves 8–10 people
Difficulty Easy

Description

"Pick Me Up" — and It Will

Tiramisu, which translates literally as "pick me up," is the great Italian dessert of contradictions: no oven, no heat, barely any cooking — yet the result is one of the most luxurious things you can put on a plate. Crisp savoiardi biscuits are dipped in strong espresso and layered with a billowing cream of egg yolks, sugar, and mascarpone that firms up overnight into something that slices clean and melts on contact.

This is the classic preparation: no cream cheese shortcuts, no heavy cream in the mascarpone filling, no alcohol (though a splash of Marsala or rum is a worthy addition). What you get is the real thing — airy, espresso-dark, and dusted with a cloud of bitter cocoa that ties everything together.

Ingredients

What You'll Need

  • 400 g savoiardi (ladyfinger biscuits)
  • 6 large eggs, separated
  • 120 g caster sugar
  • 500 g mascarpone, room temp
  • 300 ml freshly brewed espresso, cooled
  • 2 tbsp Marsala wine (optional)
  • 1 tsp pure vanilla extract
  • Pinch of fine salt
  • 3 tbsp unsweetened cocoa powder, for dusting
  • Dark chocolate shavings, to finish (optional)

Method

Step by Step

  1. Brew your espresso and let it cool completely. If using Marsala, stir it in now. Pour into a wide, shallow bowl for dipping. Set aside.

  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the egg yolks and caster sugar using a hand or stand mixer until the mixture is very pale, thick, and ribbons from the whisk — about 4–5 minutes. This step is key to the texture of the final cream.

  3. Add the mascarpone and vanilla extract to the yolk mixture. Fold gently with a spatula until completely smooth and no streaks remain. Do not beat vigorously — you want to preserve the airiness.

  4. In a separate, very clean bowl, whisk the egg whites with the pinch of salt to stiff, glossy peaks. Fold the whites into the mascarpone mixture in three additions, being careful not to deflate them. The final cream should be light and billowing.

  5. Working one at a time, dip each savoiardo briefly in the espresso — about 1 second per side. You want them moist but not waterlogged. Arrange a single layer in a 33×23 cm dish (or a similar deep serving dish).

  6. Spread half the mascarpone cream over the first layer of biscuits in a smooth, even layer. Add another layer of dipped savoiardi on top, then cover with the remaining cream, smoothing the surface flat.

  7. Cover tightly with cling film and refrigerate for at least 6 hours, but preferably overnight. Just before serving, dust generously with cocoa powder through a fine sieve. Add chocolate shavings if desired. Slice and serve cold.
