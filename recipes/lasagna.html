Classic
Lasagna

Prep Time 45 min
Cook Time 1 hr 15 min
Serves 8 people
Difficulty Intermediate

Description

A Labour of Love Worth Every Minute

Lasagna is one of those dishes that earns its reputation honestly. Layer upon layer — silky béchamel, a deeply-flavoured beef and pork ragù, tender pasta sheets, and an avalanche of melted mozzarella — it is architectural in the best possible way. This isn't the rushed weeknight version. This is the Sunday centrepiece your family will ask for by name.

The secret is patience. Let the ragù simmer low and slow until it deepens into something almost jammy, and build your béchamel with cold milk for a lump-free result. The reward is a lasagna that holds its shape when sliced and glistens when it hits the plate.

Ingredients

What You'll Need

  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 500 g ground beef
  • 250 g Italian sausage
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 4 garlic cloves, minced
  • 800 g crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp dried basil
  • 60 g unsalted butter
  • 60 g plain flour
  • 700 ml whole milk
  • Pinch of freshly grated nutmeg
  • 400 g fresh mozzarella
  • 80 g Parmigiano-Reggiano, grated
  • Salt & black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tbsp olive oil

Method

Step by Step

  1. Heat the olive oil in a wide, heavy-based pot over medium-high heat. Add the onion and cook for 5–6 minutes until soft and translucent. Add the garlic and cook for a further minute.

  2. Add the ground beef and sausage, breaking up with a wooden spoon, and cook until browned all over — about 8 minutes. Season generously with salt and pepper.

  3. Stir in the tomato paste and cook for 2 minutes. Add the crushed tomatoes, oregano, and basil. Reduce heat to low, cover partially, and simmer for 45 minutes, stirring occasionally. The sauce should be thick and fragrant.

  4. While the ragù simmers, make the béchamel: melt the butter in a saucepan over medium heat. Whisk in the flour and cook for 1 minute. Gradually add cold milk, whisking continuously, until the sauce is smooth and coats a spoon. Season with salt, pepper, and nutmeg. Remove from heat.

  5. Cook the lasagna noodles in well-salted boiling water until just al dente (about 2 minutes less than package instructions). Drain and lay flat on oiled baking paper.

  6. Preheat your oven to 190°C (375°F). In a large 33×23 cm baking dish, spread a thin layer of ragù across the base. Add a layer of noodles, then ragù, then béchamel, then torn mozzarella. Repeat three more times, finishing with a generous layer of béchamel and all the Parmigiano.

  7. Cover with foil and bake for 45 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for a further 20–25 minutes until the top is blistered and golden. Rest for 15 minutes before slicing and serving.
